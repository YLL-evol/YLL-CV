## Circadian gene evolution in Lepidoptera

Jan – June 2024 	Supervisors : Violaine Llaurens, Joséphine Ledamoisel, Manuela Lopez-Villavicencio. Collège de France/Muséum National d’Histoire Naturelle

Question:  	The activity time of Lepidoptera differs between clades and sometimes differs between sexes within a species. Is there a positive selection on circadian genes?

** Methods: **  	
Braker: Annotation of genome
MAFFT: Aligning nuleotide or proteine sequences
Iqtree: Phylogenetics
My script in R: Study the structure of gene
PALM: Modelisation of positive selection

## Human vocal and facial attractiveness

Jan – June 2023 	Supervisors : Michel Raymond, Melissa Barkat-Defradas, Claire Berticat. ISEM Institut des sciences de l’évolution de Montpellier, Lab Anthropologie Évolutive

Question: 	What we eat changes our physiology through hormones and then changes our voice and face. Does diet have an influence on vocal and facial attractiveness?

** Methods: 	**
Food survey: collection of subjects’ and judges’ diets
Glycemic load table: calculation of blood sugar as a proxy of hormones from food
Audacity & Praat + my script: phonetic analysis, extrait voice parameters
JS, HTML, PHP, SQL: presentation of voices and faces to judges
GLM in R: modeling to find the most important factors

Communications: 	European Human Behaviour and Evolution Association 2023 in London; Clinical Phonetics Days in Toulouse.

## Black Soldier Fly polygenous assortative mating

April-July 2021 	Supervisor : Christophe Bressac. IRBI, UMR 7261 CNRS, Tours, France

Question: 	Black soldier flies are high in protein and mass-raised as fodder. To better manage their reproduction, is there assortative mating?

** Methods: 	** 
Image J and my script: morphometric measurement as an index of quality in sexual selection
Marking system: my creation, monitoring individuals and their matings
Fine dissection: highlighting spermathecae
Influorescent staining: counting sperms

Communications:  	Sperm management along multiple mating in males and females of the Black Soldier Fly (Hermetia illucens) Venon*, Yang* et al. (2024) submitted to Entomologia experimentalis et applicata (first co-author)
15th International Ecology & Behaviour in Strasbourg； 2022 International Branch Meeting of Entomological Society of America
